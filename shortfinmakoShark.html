Shortfin Mako Shark

Price: $14,240

The shortfin mako shark has a vertically extended tail and a cylindrical body. With bright metallic blue colour dorsally and white ventrally, this species displays countershading. Bigger specimens frequently have darker colouring that extends to body areas that would normally be white in smaller ones. The juvenile mako differs in that it bears a clear blackish stain on the tip of the snout. The shortfin mako shark and the longfin mako shark are strikingly similar, however the longfin mako shark has larger pectoral fins, darker rather than lighter colouring around its mouth, and larger eyes. The mako may be distinguished from the closely related porbeagle sharks of the genus Lamna by having just one lateral keel on the tail and no lateral cusps on the teeth.

Shortfin mako sharks are active swimmers that frequently follow the tide into intertidal mudflats to hunt for food, which consists primarily of clams, spoon worms, crabs, shrimp, bony fish, and fish eggs. The majority of these sharks prefer to stay there rather than make extensive migrations, which has caused genetic divergence amongst populations of sharks living in various locations.
